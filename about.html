Solar Energy

Photovoltaic

Uses sunlight to generate electricity via photovoltaic panels. The output depends on how much sunlight the panels receive and how efficiently they convert it.

E = Area × η × Irradiance × PR
  • Area — panel surface in m²
  • η — panel efficiency (0–1)
  • Irradiance — daily solar resource (kWh/m²)
  • PR — performance ratio (0.70–0.85)
🌬️

Wind Energy

Turbine

Wind turbines extract kinetic energy from moving air. Output scales with the cube of wind speed, so even small speed increases yield large gains.

P = ½ × ρ × A × v³ × Cp × η
  • ρ — air density (1.225 kg/m³ at sea level)
  • A — rotor swept area (π × r²)
  • v — wind speed (m/s)
  • Cp — power coefficient (max 0.593 Betz)
  • η — generator efficiency
